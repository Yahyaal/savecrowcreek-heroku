Crow Creek is on the State of Oklahoma’s list of impaired waters. We are building a community of people who will work together to revive the creek. There are several easy actions that Crow Creek residents and lawn care providers can take to help the creek.

The Story

The Oklahoma Conservation Commission’s Water Quality Division has designated Crow Creek as its first priority watershed in an urban area and is developing a plan to improve the creek’s water quality, which has degraded over several years. Currently, Crow Creek is listed by the State as an impaired waterbody for both fish and wildlife propagation, dissolved oxygen, and primary body contact – recreation. This means that few fish and benthic macroinvertebrates are found living in the creek, and people should avoid getting in the water. Through its Blue Thumb Water Quality Education and Volunteer Program, the Commission will support volunteers to educate citizens and businesses in and around the watershed on how they can help improve the water quality. There will also be monies available to set up demonstration areas to showcase best management practices for landscaping along the creek banks. This effort will take several years; the degradation didn’t happen overnight, the recovery won’t either. Blue Thumb volunteers have monitored the health of Crow Creek since 1995 by collecting water samples for chemical testing each month, collecting benthic macroinvertebrates twice a year (winter and summer), and assessing the habitat and collecting fish once every five years. The habitat of the exposed creek is fine, but most of the aquatic life is missing from the stream.
